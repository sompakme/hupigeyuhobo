    VIN is an acronym for Vehicle Identification Number. Every Audi car in the world today has a unique identifier code called a VIN. The VIN is made up of 17 characters– a mixture of letters and numbers unique to each car.

    This vehicle identification number contains vital information about the car. Information such as its manufacturer, year of production, the plant it was produced in, type of engine, model, and more.

    When someone wants to buy a car, it is possible to check the VIN on an online database to ensure that the vehicle was not stolen, damaged, or illegally modified. 

    The VIN is usually written in a specific format that is globally recognized. This format was developed by the ISO institute. Every car manufacturer is obligated to identify all its vehicles in this special format.

    Where is the VIN found?

    Different car manufacturers put the VIN on different parts of their vehicles. The most common location across various models is on the dashboard on the driver’s side. It is usually written low on the windshield.

    Usually very close to where the windshield and the dashboard meet. It is written in such a way that looking at the VIN from the outside gives the best view. Here are the specific places where you can find the Vehicle Identification Number on your Audi.

    • Driver’s Side Door – The VIN is written on the doorjamb or the door post of the driver’s side. 
    • With the Wheels – The rear wheel well contains the VIN, just directly above the tire
    • Underneath the spare tire – The VIN is also found under the spare tire.
    • Front of the Engine Block – Once you open the hood, the VIN is written on the engine block.

    Additionally, you can also check your owner’s manual for your VIN. Even if the manual itself may probably not have the VIN recorded, it will certainly be able to tell you where to find it.

    Comprehensive VIN Decoder Guide

    We have established that the VIN is made up of 17 characters containing a lot of information. The Audi VINs are broken down into 9 sections, with each section containing a unique code of information. 

    The sections are not evenly divided since 3 or more characters can make a section and another section may consist of one character. Let’s take a closer look at the characters and the information that they give us.

    1-3rd character – Manufacturer Identifier. These three characters represent the first section. This section contains all the information about the manufacturer, the plant of manufacture as well as the country of manufacture.

    4th character – Make/Series. The 4th character makes up the 2nd section. This section tells us the make and particular series of the car.

    5th character – Engine Type. The 5th character stands alone in the 3rd section and tells us about the type of engine in the car.

    6th character – Restraint System. The 6th character stands alone in the 4th section and tells us about the type of restraint system available in the car.

    7-8th character – Model. The 5th section is made up of the 7th and 8th characters. They represent the model of the car. 

    9th character – Check Digit. This particular character marks the validity of the VIN. It is the 6th section.

    10th character – Model Year. This section indicates the model year.

    11th character – Assembly Plant. This character tells us the plant where the car was assembled.

    12-17th character – Sequential Production Numbers. The last section is made up of the last 6 characters (6 digits) that are unique for each car.

    Some prefer to divide the 17 characters into three segments;

  • The first group is the World Manufacturer Index (WMI) which covers the first three VIN symbols.
  • The second group is known as the Vehicle Descriptor Section. This section includes all VIN characters in the 4th to 8th position.
  • The third segment in the VIN contains characters placed between the 10th and 17th positions in the VIN. You may know this arrangement as the Vehicle Identifier Section.

    • The 9th character which is the check digit stands alone.

    Having seen this, the next Audi VIN you see won’t look like a bunch of strange-looking digits anymore.
