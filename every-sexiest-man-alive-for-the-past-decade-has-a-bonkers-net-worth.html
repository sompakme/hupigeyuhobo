    People magazine has been crowning an annual "Sexiest Man Alive" for the past 37 years.

    People magazine's first "Sexiest Man Alive" was Mel Gibson, selected in 1985 .

    Readers and fans of celebrity news often wonder how the "Sexiest Man Alive" is chosen from the many attractive options in any given year.

    In 2012, People editor Julia Jordan shed light on how People magazine settles upon the "Sexiest Man Alive".

    Jordan revealed that in addition to looks, staff pay close attention to reader sentiment and interest throughout the year. They also consult female celebrities and various journalists to formally crown the year's "Sexiest Man Alive."

    The celebrities chosen for "Sexiest Man Alive" by People magazine are not merely selected for their good looks and wealth, but also for the impact they have made on society.
