    Vincent Riotta Early Career Experiences

    Vincent Riotta started his acting career in theater. He trained at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art (RADA) in London and thereafter joined the Royal Shakespeare Company.     Riotta made his film debut in 1984 in the movie “The Bounty” directed by Roger Donaldson. He also played the role of Edward Young, one of the crew members on board the HMS Bounty. The film starred Mel Gibson and Anthony Hopkins. He also made impressions on several television shows in the 1980s, including “Minder,” “Bergerac,” and “The Bill.” He played guest roles on these shows before landing a regular role on the drama series “Casualty” in 1990.

    In 1990, he moved to Hollywood and started operating in the American film and television industry. He emerged in different movies such as “Johnny Stecchino,” “The Dark Half,” and “Bugsy.” He also emerged on famous TV shows like “ER,” “The Sopranos,” and “24.”

    However, in 2005, Riotta made his directorial debut with the film “Bridging the Gap.” The film was based on his own adventures growing up in an Italian-American family in New York City. The film was screened at different film festivals and obtained positive reviews.
