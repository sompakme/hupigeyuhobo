According to reports, the 28-year-old starlet has $84 million worth of real estate, including a variety of residences, from a $20 million Tribeca penthouse to an 11,000-square-foot historical landmark in California.

    Swift is the queen of changing herself, from her endearing country anthems to her nasty songs to her most recent confectionery concerts. The celebrity has moved quickly between chic Manhattan penthouses, California-cool cribs, and New England estates. Swift, though, has never lost her appeal, no matter in which she has resided. For a closer look at the star's characteristics, see below.

    Pennsylvania Home

    The Pennsylvania home where Taylor Swift grew up is for just about $1 million. At this Georgian Colonial mansion at 78 Grandview Boulevard in Wyomissing, Pennsylvania, Taylor Swift spent a significant portion of her formative years. This exquisite setting undoubtedly sparked Swift's interest in real estate with its spacious entryway and sun-drenched study, where she reputedly performed guitar as a child. Swift relocated to Nashville when she was 14 years old, but in 2013, she listed her family's house for a whopping $700,000.

    Massive Tribeca Penthouse

    She purchased the 8,300 square-foot penthouse from Lord of the Rings filmmaker Peter Jackson, who had completely renovated it to better connect the two levels of houses. For $47.7 million, the singer who wore the most expensive dresses on red carpet has purchased an entire block in New York City. In 2014, she paid $19.95 million for two neighboring penthouses in a Tribeca building, marking her first real estate acquisition in New York City.     Country Beginnings

    Flashback to 2011when Swift had been embroiled in her infamous rivalry with Kanye West for years as she embarked on her Speak Now world tour. As her fame soared, the country singer lavished $2.5 million on a 5,601-square-foot estate in Nashville. The grand house had a sparkling pool, four bathrooms, and a half bath. Located a little bit south and west of downtown Nashville, she spent an additional $1,400,000 for a historic red brick mini-mansion next to a busy main road.

    West Village Apartment

    This brick townhouse, built-in 1912, has been renovated in contemporary style. The area contains a sizable indoor pool, five bedrooms, and seven bathrooms. Double-height ceilings, vintage Parisian brickwork, and a selection of opulent chandeliers are luxurious elements. One of the most exquisite homes features a rooftop patio, an outdoor kitchen, a jacuzzi, and a built-in BBQ. The West Village townhouse that the singer who's worth millions formerly rented at 23 Cornelia Street has seen a significant increase in demand since the publication of her most recent album, Lover.

    Neighboring Townhouse

    Swift moved horizontally by buying the century-old townhouse next door just three years after purchasing the two Tribeca penthouses. The house has upscale features like a home theater, gym, guest suite, and terrace. According to the New York Post, the award-winning singer spent $18 million for a brand-new Tribeca property only one house away from her own. Former French ambassador Dominique Strauss-Kahn, who was placed under house arrest in 2011, had previously owned the apartment. Leopoldo Rosati, a renowned architect, and designer, created a stunning design for the four-bedroom townhouse.

    Beverly Hills Property

    The singer who has sold millions of albums has owned three residences in Los Angeles, the most magnificent of which is her mansion in Beverly Hills, built-in 1934. Swift decided to make the 10,982-square-foot estate into a historical landmark when she bought it from movie producer Samuel Goldwyn in 2015. Swift resides primarily on the East Coast but occasionally visits Los Angeles. She sold two of her Beverly Hills residences in 2018. the other is a smaller single-story home she utilized as a guesthouse for $2.65 million. The first was a Cape Cod-style cottage and cost $4 million.

    Watch Hill Seaside Estate

    Taylor Swift's famous A-list parties site is her 12,000-square-foot beach house, located on the highest point of the Rhode Island town. One of her best purchases was home with seven bedrooms, eight fireplaces, a gorgeous pool, and views of the 700-foot-long shoreline. Her claimed payment for the massive $17.75 million acquisition was all cash. The singer gets unrestricted views of the lake from the property's 5 acres of land and more than 700 feet of oceanfront on top of Watch Hill.

    Nashville Condo

    Taylor Swift made her real estate debut at 20, investing in a 3,240-square-foot Music Row condo in Nashville. The industrial-style penthouse has a large open floor plan and floor-to-ceiling windows. One of her earliest real estate acquisitions was worth $1.9 million.     Beverly Hills Property

    The Georgian-style residence boasts six bedrooms, seven bathrooms, and a stunning view of the lush California environment from when it was created. Despite its unassuming exterior, the four-bedroom, four-bath ranch has white vaulted ceilings, floor-to-ceiling glass, and—best of all—a climate-controlled wine cellar with 1000 bottles.     Greek Renaissance Estate

    Taylor Swift bought a Greek renaissance mansion for her second Nashville home, despite leading a jet-set lifestyle. With a 5,600 square-foot main house and a 2,000 square-foot guesthouse, the exquisitely designed property has vaulted ceilings, herringbone floors, and marble fireplaces. In June 2011, she bought a new home in Nashville for $2.5 million, this time in the peaceful Forest Hills neighborhood. The Greek Revival-style estate has four bedrooms, four and a half bathrooms, a guest house next to a large outdoor pool, and other amenities.
