    Danielle Pinnock is an American actress, writer, and comedian popularly known for being the main cast member on the CBS series Ghosts which premiered on October 7, 2021. In the series she plays the 1920’s Prohibition era jazz singer “Alberta.”

    Danielle Pinnock Career, Awards And Achievements

    Danielle is actress, writer, and comedian who after moving to Los Angeles in 2016 was cast as an understudy for the Robert O’Hara play Barbecue. Later she was cast in her first television role on NBC’s This Is Us. Danielle created the Instagram series Hashtag Booked in 2018, to comment on the barriers faced by Black women in the entertainment industry. She and co-creator LaNisa Renée Frederick received a Webby Award honor for the series.

    On the 2021 CBS comedy Ghosts, Danielle is a main cast member on which she plays Alberta, a 1920s era jazz singer. Despite the fact that she has no formal vocal training, she began to take lessons after she was cast.
