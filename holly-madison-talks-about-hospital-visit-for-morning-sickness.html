    Holly Madison is A-OK after being hospitalized for a severe case of morning sickness.

    “When it started happening really late [in my pregnancy] and I was really vomiting a lot during the day, it freaked me out,” the 33-year-old told Entertainment Tonight Tuesday (Dec. 4). “I was told to go to the doctor and get an IV and everything. So, that was kind of scary to me just because it seemed like something so unusual, something that I hadn’t read about. But everything’s OK so far, so that’s good.

    “I’m excited to get through these last months of pregnancy. Hopefully no more scares,” she added.

    The blonde bombshell is seven months along with her first daughter to Electric Daisy Carnival founder Pasquale Rotella. The doctor has ordered her to take it easy till her baby arrives in March. “I was threatened with bed rest,” she admitted. “I haven’t been given bed rest yet, but they’re like, ‘You better take it easy and don’t lift anything over 5 lbs.'”

    Holly also had well wishes for another mama-to-be, the Duchess of Cambridge Kate Middleton. “I wish her the best and I’m glad she’s getting the best possible care and I’m super excited for her,” Madison said. “I think everybody wants to see [the baby] named Diana if it’s a girl. I think that would melt the hearts of the world.”

    No word if she wished her former flame Hugh Hefner a congrats on his recent engagement.
